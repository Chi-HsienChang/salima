Thank you for visiting Salima's website!

I am a PhD candidate at Taiwan Evolutionary Intelligence Laboratory (TEILab) advised by Dr. Tian-Li Yu in Electrical Engineering at National Taiwan University (NTU).

Before joining TEILab, I did my Master's degree in Statistics at NTU and my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics Sciences at the National Chengchi University. My research focuses on developing theoretical support for model-based genetic algorithms, aiming to bridge the gap between theoretical understanding and practical applications of these algorithms in solving complex optimization problems.

From February to August 2023, I interned at the USDA, contributing to the development of a Linkout plugin for JBrowse 2 and a machine learning model for predicting images in insect databases using single nucleotide polymorphisms. Recently, I have been interning at the NII from February to April 2024, focusing on generating molecular graphs through model-based genetic algorithms. Subsequently, I will be a visiting student at MIT from June 2024 to May 2025, where I'll work on advancing fully interpretable simulators for pre-mRNA splicing.

I would be delighted to discuss research further or receive any feedback you might have. Please feel free to reach out to me via email at salimachchang@gmail.com.
